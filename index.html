Why I Went to VidCon 2015 by: the Tim Ridenour

In 2014 I was in the digital cinematography program at Full Sail University. I went to film school not to become an actor, or a director. I went to become a producer. To be a good producer you have to know how to do everything, and in film school you are required to do everything.

One of the requirements for the program is creating a youtube channel to post are homework assignment on. This could be anything from doing sound design, editing, or directing a commercial. I was fine with all of this, because I'm a behind the camera kind of guy. But I knew the time was going to come, that I had to step infront of the camera. I didn't have a choice about it. It was part of getting my degree.

Knowing I was going to have to get in front of the camera I decided to start making youtube videos. The problem was I didn't know what type of videos to make. One day a video my Furious Pete was recommended to me. I've never heard of him before this, but I really enjoyed the video. Pete was trying to make a free standing loop out of Pringles. I knew that was going to be my first video. It was supprisingly difficult to complete this feet. It took me over 6 hours under hot studio lights I setup in my room. I got around three views in a week.

I have a brand new channel with one video and three views, and made the decision that if I get to 100 subscribers in the next three and a half months, I would go to VidCon in California the next year. I thought 'there's no way this is going to happen.' But I wanted to give myself a challenge. The next day I shot and posted my second video. It was taking on the cinnamon challenge, since that what was popular at the time. The next morning the video had over two-thousand views, and I was already at 48 subscribers. In two months I had close to 300 subscribers, and decided that I would go to PlayList Live in Florida that November, and to VidCon the next year.
